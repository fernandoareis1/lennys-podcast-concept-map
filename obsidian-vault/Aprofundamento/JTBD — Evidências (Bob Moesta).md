# 📜 JTBD — Evidências (Bob Moesta)

**↑ Card:** [[Jobs to be Done (JTBD)]] · **Tema:** [[Discovery — Jobs to be Done (teoria da demanda)]] · **Camada:** L4 (Aprofundamento)
**Fonte:** [[Bob Moesta]] — *How to find work you love* (1:24:56)

> Esta é a camada densa: citações longas, mecânica completa e nuances. Para a versão curta, fique no [[Jobs to be Done (JTBD)|card]].

## 1. Contexto e resultado, não dor e ganho
A maior confusão sobre JTBD, segundo Moesta, é tratá-lo como "pain and gain" em vez de **"context and outcome"**:

> "One of the biggest misconceptions around Jobs to Be Done is this notion that it's pain and gain as opposed to context and outcome. (…) the context makes the irrational rational. So the moment you hear a story and you go 'I can't believe that', nine times out of 10 it's because you don't have the rest of the story." *(00:00 / 54:21)*

A escola de negócios ensina "dor e ganho", mas o que dá valor é o **contexto**: "It's not that I'm in pain, but it's the context that makes me value this in the moment that much more than something else." *(10:34)*

## 2. Contratar para fazer progresso
> "People hire products, they don't buy them — they hire them to make progress in their life." *(05:31)*

Isso reposiciona a concorrência pelo **lado da demanda**: um Snickers (fome + muito trabalho pela frente) compete com café, Red Bull e um sanduíche; um Milky Way (regular após algo emocional, sozinho) compete com uma taça de vinho ou uma corrida. "Jobs helps you see the true competitive set from the demand side, as opposed to the supply side." *(06:49–07:29)*

## 3. A causalidade: supply ≠ demand
> "Most people think they create a product and that creates demand. But (…) a struggling moment causes demand." *(07:44)*

O struggling moment pode existir por anos sem solução. **Estudar a causa da mudança** (não a inércia) é onde a inovação acontece: "Most people are studying the momentum of where people are (…) what we're trying to do is study what causes people to change their direction. That's where innovation happens." *(55:37)*

## 4. As 6 fases da jornada de compra
Moesta quebra a decisão em seis fases — e a venda deve respeitar **onde** a pessoa está:

1. **First thought**
2. **Passive looking** (problem-aware, solution-unaware)
3. **Active looking** (problem- e solution-aware)
4. **Deciding** (fazer trade-offs)
5. **First use**
6. **Ongoing use** (formar o novo hábito)

> "Where is the customer in their timeline of buying? (…) you have a timeline of how you want to sell to them and after the demo you try to close, but what if they're actually in passive looking and want a demo to learn more?" *(16:37–17:25)*

## 5. As três fontes de energia
Toda decisão tem energia **funcional** (tempo, esforço, conhecimento), **emocional** (como me sinto) e **social** (como os outros me veem):

> "There's functional energy — time, space, effort, knowledge. There's emotional energy — how I feel. And then there's social — how I want others to perceive me." *(34:26–35:11)*

## 6. O que dizem ≠ o que querem
> "93% said they wanted an Energy Star compliant house. (…) nobody bought it, they all bought the finished basement." *(17:25–17:48)*

Por isso a pesquisa não é survey: é **"criminal and intelligence interrogation that feels like therapy"** *(38:34)* — você reconstrói o que de fato aconteceu, não o que a pessoa racionaliza.

## 7. Quantas entrevistas
Os padrões (sets de pushes/pulls/anxieties/habits) começam a se repetir em ~7–8; ele faz 10–12. "I would rather do two rounds of 12 interviews than do 24 interviews." *(29:07)* → detalhes em [[Entrevista — Playbook (Moesta + Torres)]].

## 8. Os 3 takeaways do próprio Moesta
> "Struggling moments is the key (…). Think about the progress people are trying to make — what is *their* standard, not yours (…). And choose what to suck at — make sure your trade-offs map the trade-offs of the customer. Nine times out of 10, most products that fail is because they made a trade-off that the customer didn't agree with." *(58:14)*
