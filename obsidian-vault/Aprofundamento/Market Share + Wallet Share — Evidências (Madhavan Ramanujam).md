## A tese: dois motores
> "If you want to build an enduring business, you need to master two engines: market share and wallet share. (…) for gaining both, you need to be good at acquisition, monetization, and retention — get customers, make initial money on them, but also make money on an ongoing basis and have them refer more." *(07:08–08:17)*

O erro é a **estratégia de motor único**: "I'll grow at all costs and postpone monetization", ou monetizar cedo e perder aquisição. "You don't fly an aircraft on one engine. Why do that for your business?" *(08:17–09:21)*

## Os arquétipos e as 6 armadilhas
- **Disruptor:** (1) *land but don't expand* — deu a fazenda no preço de entrada e não tem para onde crescer; (2) confunde market share **ganho** com market share **mantido** (foco em adquirir, esquece de reter/upsell).
- **Moneymaker:** (1) *nickel-and-dime* — taxas escondidas, cobrar por tudo; (2) *price premium paradox* — preço tão alto que mata a aquisição.
- **Community builder:** (1) *foundation vs frontier* — tão focado na base leal que esquece de atrair novos; (2) **treina o cliente a esperar mais por menos**.

> "Being a profitable growth architect means avoiding these traps — being a disruptor, a moneymaker, and a community builder all at the same time." *(09:47–11:46)*

## Pricing baseado em outcome (o quadrante a perseguir)
Um 2x2 de **autonomia × atribuição**: o quadrante alvo (alta autonomia + alta atribuição) é o **outcome-based pricing**.

> "About 5% of companies are probably in a true outcome-based pricing model. If you want to win in AI, figure out a way to get to that quadrant." *(00:33)* → [[Pricing baseado em outcome]]

## Monetização desde o dia 1 (IA)
> "The winners in AI will need to master monetization from day one. If you start training your customers to expect $20 a month and you anchored yourself on a low price point, you're in trouble. (…) 20% of what you build drives 80% of the willingness to pay — but the irony is that 20% is the easiest thing to build often." *(00:11–00:30)* → [[Willingness to Pay (regra 20-80)]]

A tese do livro anterior (*Monetizing Innovation*): construir produtos que as pessoas **precisam, valorizam e estão dispostas a pagar** — conversando sobre willingness to pay **antes** de construir. *(05:21–06:38)*
